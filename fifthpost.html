Socioeconomic Status includes income, educational attainment and occupation. These factors are one of the major driving forces of prevalence of diseases. Educational attainment affects your occupation which in turn affects your income. In the end, all these factors play a role in being exposed to some types of diseases like Type 2 Diabetes. In this article, the authors discuss the effect of socioeconomic status of Parents and Spouses on 21st century women in the United States from infancy to adulthood and how these factors affect their prevalence of diabetes and obesity.

Due to income inequality in the USA and the world at large, some families are more exposed to diseases while others are not. High income families have the means to provide their family with good nutrition, medication, and good lifestyle practices that improves good health and most importantly they can acquire the luxury of good healthcare from top physicians. This is not the case for low income families. WHY? The answer is simple. Low income limits quality of life.

Low income families in most cases may not be able to afford bills, good food, good lifestyle activities and others. Due to this, they may suffer from stress and its related illness. As inferred from the article, low income families have a high chance of getting diabetes as compared to high income ones. By comparing the income/socioeconomic status of parents of these children, we see that the inequalities in the income leaves children from low income earning families at a higher risk of contracting diabetes. After marriage, the income/socioeconomic status of their spouses also played an important role in contracting the disease and this was consistent with the trend when they were younger.
